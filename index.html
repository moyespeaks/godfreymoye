The Story

More Than A Speaker.

Since 2007

Godfrey Moye doesn't just speak — he enters the room differently. As an actor trained by the Upright Citizens Brigade, a licensed minister, and a mentoring program founder, he brings a rare combination: someone who understands story, knows Scripture, and has actually walked alongside the people he speaks to.

Based in New York City, Godfrey serves as Youth Minister at Lower Manhattan Community Church, where he leads the LMY youth group and runs the Life on Purpose Program — a mentoring initiative for teens built around self-discovery, cooperative learning, and intentional living.

His approach isn't motivational fluff. It's grounded in precision, preparation, and presence — the same qualities that make an actor compelling, a preacher powerful, and a mentor trustworthy.

Signature Program

Teens Living On Purpose.

The Life on Purpose Program isn't a one-day assembly. It's a sustained experience that walks teens through the questions that actually matter: Who am I? What do I love? What does the world need from me?

Through cooperative learning structures, guided self-reflection, and real conversation — not worksheets — participants leave with a clearer sense of identity and a concrete direction to move toward.

The program has been delivered in New York City schools, youth groups, and community organizations, with results that show up not in test scores, but in how students carry themselves after.

The Ikigai Framework — Built into the curriculum is the Japanese concept of Ikigai: the intersection of what you love, what you're good at, what the world needs, and what you can be paid for. Teens don't just learn about purpose. They locate it.
